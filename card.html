Gray wolf

The gray wolf is one of the world's best-known and most-researched animals, with probably more books written about it than any other wildlife species.

  • Scientific Name: Canis lupus
  • Weight: males averaging 43–45 kg (95–99 lb) and females 36–38.5 kg (79–85 lb).
  • Diet: It feeds primarily on large ungulates.
  • Habitat: The gray wolf is a habitat generalist, and can occur in deserts, grasslands, forests and arctic tundras.
